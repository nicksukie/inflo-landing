Taking a hard stance on malicious content from the start Building a platform with quality in mind

Countering the spread of misinformation

Social media was never meant to handle news. That's why so many platforms today are allocating incredible amounts of time and effort to manually controlling the quality of the information they help to spread. But this is not an effective solution, and false information continues to spread abundantly via social media1. Meanwhile, controversies about who or what should be allowed on social platforms arise on a regular basis, making clear the need for an alternative, responsible place to and consume—and share—news. That's why we've taken an automated approach to label any content deemed potentially untrustworty, in order expedite the process of propagating reliable information.

Developing a holistic view of reliability

Fake, or fabricated news is only the tip of the iceberg when it comes to low-quality content. So instead of using naive methods as binary true/false labeling or underhandedly hiding certain content from view, we're developing machine learning models to detect low-quality information on multiple dimensions, resulting in a set of content "nutrition facts" intended to offer users objective insights enabling them to make informed decisions on the content they choose to read. What's more, the results of our algorithms—including accuracy—will be presented in an entirely transparent, objective way, and subject to review—because we believe a social platform should be responsible for the integrity of the content it serves.
